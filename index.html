watchlist
Companies doing really cool things in space and aviation (please hire me!!)
  1. ispace is a Japanese lunar robotic exploration company, working under NASA's Commercial Lunar Payload Services (CLPS) contract to land on the lunar surface and scout for water and other resources. Their first mission to the lunar surface the Hakuto-R M1 ended up in loss of communication during the final descent, resulting in a hard landing. The Hakuto-R M2 is currently in orbit heading to the Moon.
  2. Boom Supersonics is building a supersonic commercial aircraft. Supersonic commercial travel ended with the demise of the British/French Concorde aircraft in 2003 due to two tragic accidents and the high cost of development and maintenance. Boom's Overture intends to bring back supersonic travel by 2027. Boom recently completed it's first supersonic flight test of the XB-1 (or Baby Boom), a one-third scale demonstrator aircraft. If this happens I'll be able to reach my home country in around 8 hours (instead of 24).
  3. Digantara is an Indian space startup building a catalogue of Space Situational Awareness data to provide insights for safe and sustainable space operations. As LEO grows congested and contested, and the fear of space debris lingers overhead, the need of such "maps" to track objects and debris, is crucial to continue utilization of space to improve life on Earth.
  4. Planet Labs needs no introduction. If SpaceX democratized our access to space, Planet revolutionized the way we observed the Earth from orbit. Planet builds cheap, reliable and frequent launched satellites that image the Earth daily and enable real time monitoring for a multitude of applications
  5. Hermeus is an Atlanta based company building a commercial hypersonic aircraft intended to fly around Mach 5. Similar to Boom, this aircraft when built, will revolutionize the way we travel.
